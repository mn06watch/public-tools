The ERIC Objection

In Episode 45, Mitchell and her guest Heather Honey discuss ERIC — the Electronic Registration Information Center, the bipartisan voter roll system that 33 states used to catch duplicate registrations and dead voters. Their objection wasn't fraud. It was voter registration.

Episode 45 · 9:08
"Eligible but unregistered"
"They were very, very interested in getting records associated with people that they call eligible but unregistered. And so these are people who have declined to register when they interacted with a particular agency. So maybe they went to get their driver's license or photo ID, and they were asked at the time, would you like to register to vote? And these people said, no."
— Heather Honey

Honey describes ERIC's focus on people who declined to register to vote — not fraud, not ineligible voters, but eligible citizens who said no.

Episode 45 · ~9:08 · Heather Honey (Speaker 2)
Episode 45 · 9:25
"No thank you. I don't want to."
"And these people said, no. Thank you. I don't want to. And so these folks are then targeted, and the membership agreement requires states to reach out to those people and say, you know, come on now. Are you sure you don't want to?"
— Heather Honey

Honey uses the word "targeted" to describe ERIC contacting eligible voters about registration. The system was following federal law. She frames compliance as an attack.

Episode 45 · ~9:25 · Heather Honey (Speaker 2)
Episode 45 · 19:33
"10 times more people added than removed"
"They cause 10 times more people to be added to the roles than they could ever cause to be removed."
— Heather Honey

The core admission. ERIC caught duplicate registrations and dead voters — but it also registered eligible citizens. And that was the problem. Honey now holds a newly created position at DHS overseeing election integrity.

Episode 45 · ~19:33 · Heather Honey (Speaker 2)
Episode 45 · 20:26
"95% of them to register — each and every"
"What is mandatory is that they have to send letters to follow up to try to get these eligible but unregistered. I guess they call them EBUs, eligible but unregistered. They have to try to get 95% of them to register."
— Cleta Mitchell

Mitchell confirms: ERIC's mandatory voter outreach — contacting eligible citizens about registration — was the provision she objected to. Not fraud. Registration.

Episode 45 · ~20:26 · Cleta Mitchell (Speaker 0)
Mitchell objected to ERIC because it registered voters.
The bill she architects removes them.

The Pressure Campaign

Nine states withdrew from ERIC between 2022 and 2023. Missouri Secretary of State Jay Ashcroft's own staff privately called the conspiracy theories driving the withdrawals "horrible and misleading." On Mitchell's podcast, Ashcroft acknowledged her pressure.

Episode 15 · 17:53
"We've cussed and discussed about it"
"We've cussed and discussed about it."
— Jay Ashcroft, Missouri Secretary of State
"We did. And, but you worked hard on it and then made a decision to withdraw."
— Cleta Mitchell

Ashcroft admits Mitchell pressured him on ERIC. Mitchell confirms it. On her own podcast, to her own audience. Missouri withdrew anyway.

Episode 15 · ~17:53 · Jay Ashcroft (Speaker 1) and Cleta Mitchell (Speaker 0)

The Campus Strategy

The SAVE America Act excludes student IDs — even from state universities. Mitchell specifically called for this. At a private RNC donor retreat in 2023, audio obtained by the Washington Post captured her outlining a strategy to limit college campus voting. On her own podcast, she said the quiet part out loud.

Episode 25 · 24:33
"Feed them into the election process"
"A massive project that has been funded by the left and through our federal tax dollars to get college students on campuses to basically feed them into the election process so that there are polling places on the campuses."
— Cleta Mitchell

Mitchell frames eligible college students voting at campus polling places as a threat. The SAVE America Act does exactly what she describes wanting: it excludes student IDs, effectively eliminates same-day registration, and restricts the mail voting that students rely on.

Episode 25 · ~24:33 · Cleta Mitchell (Speaker 0)

The Contractor

Heather Honey appears at every stage of the investigation documented in "The Integrity Gap." She worked on the Arizona Cyber Ninjas audit Mitchell funded. She wrote the ERIC report Mitchell used to pressure state withdrawals. She was paid $121,284 by Mitchell's dark money fund. In August 2025, she was appointed DHS Deputy Assistant Secretary for Election Integrity — a position that didn't exist before Trump created it. If the SAVE America Act passes, she receives every state's voter rolls.

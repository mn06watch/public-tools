CONCLUSION: THE EMMER PATTERN
================================================================================

Rep. Tom Emmer does NOT lie when speaking to different audiences, but he DOES:

1. SELECT FACTS: Chooses which statistics to emphasize
   - Conservative: Fraud, state corruption, Democratic failure
   - Mainstream: Economy, wages, inflation, consumer impacts

2. ADJUST LANGUAGE: Changes intensity of phrasing
   - Conservative: "Numbnuts," "crooked," "should be in cuffs"
   - Mainstream: "Weak leader," "unreasonable," "intellectual disconnect"

3. EXPAND NARRATIVE: Adds contextual details to support audience worldview
   - Conservative: Detailed fraud, media conspiracy, political corruption
   - Mainstream: Policy mechanics, legislative process, economic data

4. RESPOND TO PUSHBACK: Becomes defensive with challengers, expansive with supporters
   - When host asks tough question: Becomes careful, qualified
   - When host is supportive: Becomes confident, detailed

5. CHANGE SCOPE: Alters what topics are central vs. peripheral
   - Conservative: State corruption is central, federal policy peripheral
   - Mainstream: Federal policy is central, state issues absent

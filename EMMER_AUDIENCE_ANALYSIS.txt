CONCLUSION
================================================================================

Rep. Tom Emmer demonstrably adapts his messaging based on audience:

CONSERVATIVE OUTLETS: More inflammatory, more attacks on Democrats and Democratic-led states,
more fraud/corruption discussion, more confident tone, fewer defenses of methodology, personal
attacks by name, broader narrative connecting Minnesota to national corruption patterns.

MAINSTREAM OUTLETS: More measured, policy-focused, defensive when challenged, acknowledges
legitimate concerns about tactics/methods, more careful language, avoids personal attacks except
when necessary, focuses on federal legislative process.

This is not a simple matter of lying - the facts (4,000 removals, body cameras funded, administrative
warrants effective) are consistent. Rather, it is selective emphasis, language intensity, scope of
discussion, and rhetorical strategy adjusted for audience belief systems and expectations.

Conservative audiences appear to expect and reward inflammatory language, attacks on Democratic
leaders, and connections to broader corruption narratives. Mainstream audiences appear to reward
policy explanation, acknowledgment of complexity, and measured language.

When challenged by mainstream hosts, Emmer becomes more defensive and careful. When hosted by
conservative interviewers, Emmer is more confident and attacks more directly.
